📚 Knowledge Library — Recommended Books for Students

Explore books across different fields to grow your curiosity, creativity, and understanding of the world.

🔬 Science Books

A Brief History of Time — Stephen Hawking

This book introduces the mysteries of the universe in a simple way and will expand your horizon about space, time, and scientific discovery.

The Magic of Reality — Richard Dawkins

Explains natural phenomena beautifully and helps students fall in love with the world of science and critical thinking.

Astrophysics for Young People — Neil deGrasse Tyson

A fun guide to understanding the cosmos that sparks curiosity about how the universe works.

🏛 History Books

Sapiens — Yuval Noah Harari

Shows how humans evolved and built civilizations, helping students understand society and human behavior.

The Diary of a Young Girl — Anne Frank

A powerful personal story that builds empathy and teaches lessons about resilience and history.

Things Fall Apart — Chinua Achebe

Provides insight into culture and history while deepening appreciation for storytelling.

🌱 Life Skills & Personal Growth

Atomic Habits — James Clear

Teaches practical strategies for building good habits and achieving success through small improvements.

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective Teens — Sean Covey

Guides students on leadership, responsibility, and making positive life choices.

Mindset — Carol Dweck

Encourages a growth mindset and helps students embrace challenges as opportunities.

💻 Technology & Innovation

Steve Jobs — Walter Isaacson

An inspiring biography that shows how creativity and persistence can change the world.

AI Superpowers — Kai-Fu Lee

Introduces students to artificial intelligence and its impact on the future.

📖 Fiction That Builds Imagination

Harry Potter Series — J.K. Rowling

Encourages imagination, courage, and the importance of friendship.

The Hobbit — J.R.R. Tolkien

A classic adventure that inspires bravery and curiosity.

Wonder — R.J. Palacio

Promotes kindness, empathy, and understanding of others.

Reading across different subjects helps students become well‑rounded thinkers and lifelong learners.
